A hibiscus I painted while quarantining with my family in Bengaluru, India. I love the light transculence here.

The challenging thing about live flowers is that they start to droop infinitesmally as soon as you pluck them from the branch. I had about two hours to get the outline of the hibiscus down before the crisp, crinkly folds sank into themselves.

Watercolor and marker on watercolor paper. January 2021.
